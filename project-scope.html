Project Overview

Project Scope

The Lavender AI project aims to develop an intelligent greenhouse management system that leverages artificial intelligence and IoT technologies to optimize lavender cultivation. Our system integrates real-time monitoring, automated control, and predictive analytics to improve crop yield and resource efficiency.

Literature Survey

Disease Detection

Deep learning CNNs perform well in labs but fail in real fields. Existing systems lack real-time IoT camera integration, simultaneous disease/insect detection, and hardware-level alerts.

Climate Control

Current IoT climate controllers rely on simple IF-ELSE hysteresis, causing temperature overshoots. No crop-specific closed-loop control exists for lavender's unique 22–28°C and 55–75% RH needs.

Nutrient & Irrigation

Commercial EC sensors drift in greenhouse conditions. Vision-only systems cannot discriminate between N-Deficiency and N-Lockout as both present identical chlorosis symptoms.

Adaptive Lighting

Commercial systems use fixed spectrums or static daily timers. They fail to adapt to real-time plant growth stages (Vegetative vs Flowering), wasting energy and reducing terpene yields.

Research Objectives

  • 1 Develop a unified AI-powered greenhouse management system integrating disease, climate, nutrient, and lighting control into a single architecture.
  • 2 Implement a YOLOv8m-based real-time detector for lavender disease and insects using ESP32-CAM live streams with a 10-frame stability buffer.
  • 3 Build a hybrid Random Forest climate controller for state-aware, closed-loop regulation of fan speeds and humidifiers.
  • 4 Deploy a MobileNetV3-Large network for 5-class nitrogen stress classification, crossing visual data with soil moisture readings.
  • 5 Integrate a VGG16-based adaptive lighting controller classifying 4 plant growth stages to adjust RGB LED spectra.
  • 6 Engineer a complete prototype ensuring total hardware costs remain under $40 per node.

Methodology (Architecture)

The proposed system follows a four-layer architecture, ensuring seamless data flow from edge devices to the AI backend and out to user applications. The AI models (MobileNetV3, VGG16) were trained using a two-phase transfer learning approach.

1. Data Acquisition Layer

Sensing, imaging, and streaming via ESP32-CAM, DHT22, DS18B20, LDR, and capacitive soil moisture sensors.

2. Intelligence Layer

AI inference and decision-making driven by YOLOv8m (Detection), Random Forest (Climate), MobileNetV3 (Nitrogen), and VGG16 (Lighting).

3. Control Layer

Actuator command dispatch controlling the 12V DC fans, humidifiers, pump relays, RGB LEDs, and buzzer alerts.

4. Application Layer

User interaction and persistence managed through a cross-platform Flutter app, Flask API, and Firebase Firestore.

Hardware Components

  • ESP32 DevKit V1: Secondary Control Node
  • DHT22: Air Temp & Humidity
  • DS18B20: Soil Temperature
  • Capacitive v2.0: Soil Moisture
  • LDR: Ambient Light Sensor
  • L298N & Relays: Motor and Pump Drivers
